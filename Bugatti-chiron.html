Bugatti Chiron

The Bugatti Chiron is a mid-engine two-seater sports car designed and developed in Germany by Bugatti Engineering GmbH[6] and manufactured in Molsheim, France by French automobile manufacturer Bugatti Automobiles S.A.S.

  • The successor to the Bugatti Veyron.
  • Chiron was first shown at the Geneva Motor Show on 1 March 2016.
  • The car is named after the Monegasque driver Louis Chiron.
